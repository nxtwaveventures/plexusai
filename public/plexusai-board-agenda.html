01
Executive Overview & Mission Clarity
⏱ 15 minutes
Problem Statement

Why Plexus AI exists

  • Healthcare AI achieves impressive lab results — real-world clinical translation rate remains <10%
  • Core bottleneck: gap between innovation and structured clinical validation
  • Root causes: absence of clinician involvement, poor workflow integration, lack of structured RWE
Unique Positioning

What makes Plexus AI different

  • Hospital-embedded CoE — not a consulting firm, not pure tech, not a hospital innovation cell
  • Active clinician-led validation using real patient data
  • Structured pathway: Pilot → Validation → Scale
  • Real-World Evidence (RWE) as the primary, monetisable deliverable
Board Discussion
What is our primary success metric at 12 months — and does the board agree on the definition?
How do we define "validation" in a way that satisfies regulators, clinicians, and investors simultaneously?
What is the minimum viable proof point to attract our first paying partner?
02
Model Design & Governance Deep Dive
⏱ 20 minutes
Core Operating Model

Five activities that drive the CoE

  • Partner Onboarding: Structured intake, scope definition, and expectations alignment
  • Clinical Deployment: Integration with OPD, IPD, ICU, and diagnostic workflows
  • Doctor Engagement: Structured feedback loops, usability testing, clinician training
  • Data Capture: Real-time usage, clinical outcomes, and efficiency metrics
  • Validation & Reporting: RWE generation, case studies, and publishable results
Board Discussion
Should Plexus AI establish a formal steering committee from day one? Who should sit on it?
How do we balance clinical independence with partner commercial interests?
What regulatory or ethical certifications should Plexus AI pursue early?
Key tension: Maintaining scientific independence while generating revenue from the partners whose tools are being validated. A clear governance charter is critical from day one.
03
Clinical Integration & Doctor Adoption
⏱ 15 minutes
The Real Challenge

Doctor buy-in

  • Doctors don't adopt tools that add friction — success means reduced workload + visible benefit
  • Integration into existing workflows (EHR, diagnostics, ward rounds) is non-negotiable
  • Pilot bias: Early adopters are not representative of broader clinical adoption potential
Phased Clinical Rollout
Pre-pilot
Weeks 1–4
Workflow mapping · clinician onboarding · baseline data collection
Pilot
Weeks 5–12
30-day limited deployment · structured feedback · interim analysis
Full Deploy
Month 4+
Expanded access · continuous RWE capture · workflow optimisation
Board Discussion
Which clinical specialties should be priority #1? (Oncology? Emergency? ICU? Diagnostics?)
Should Plexus AI hire dedicated clinical coordinators or rely on hospital staff?
What incentive structures will sustain doctor engagement beyond initial pilots?
Clinician adoption rate is the single most powerful validation signal. A tool used by 80% of doctors in a pilot is worth more commercially than perfect outcomes in 20% of cases. Adoption rate should be a headline KPI.
04
Validation Framework & Real-World Evidence
⏱ 15 minutes
What Makes RWE Credible
  • Clinical rigour: Defined outcomes, control or comparison groups where possible
  • Transparent reporting: Methodology, limitations, and conflicts of interest declared
  • Generalisability: Data representative of real populations, not just pilot cohorts
  • Independent validation: Third-party review or peer-reviewed publication
Validation Timeline
Months 1–3
Baseline data collection · outcome metric definition · initial feedback loops
Months 4–6
Interim analysis · statistical review · publication preparation
Months 9–12
Final RWE report · case study published · regulatory dossier compiled
Proposed RWE Framework
01

Define

02

Deploy

Clinical integration, data capture infrastructure

03

Analyse

Interim and final statistical analysis

04

Report

Case study, publication, regulatory dossier

Should validation be third-party audited? What certification model?

Business Model & Partnerships
⏱ 20 minutes
Revenue Streams
  • Validation Programs: Structured per-engagement fee from AI and MedTech startups for end-to-end clinical validation
  • Sponsored Research: Pharma and MedTech companies commission independent RWE studies through the embedded network
  • Grants & Partnerships: DST, BIRAC, and ICMR funding for nationally-aligned innovation programs
Partner Prioritisation
Tier 1 · Priority

Diagnostic AI

High impact · fast validation cycle · proven clinical demand

Tier 2 · Near-term

Clinical Workflow AI

EHR, notes, triage — high clinician touchpoint frequency

Tier 3 · Pipeline

MedTech Devices

Longer cycles, higher revenue per engagement

Tier 4 · Strategic

Pharma / RWE

High-value, relationship-driven, longer sales cycle

Board Discussion
Which revenue model is most sustainable — upfront fees, outcomes-based, or hybrid?
What is the realistic total addressable market in India and globally?
Should Plexus AI actively sell or build for inbound demand first?
How do we prevent conflicts of interest when validating competing products?
Key tension: More rigorous validation = longer engagement = higher cost per partner. The board needs to define the commercial vs. scientific threshold — and who sets it for each engagement.

Scalability & Multi-Centre Strategy
⏱ 15 minutes
Expansion Phases
Phase 1
0–12 Mo
Validate model in one hospital · build replicable playbook · document all processes
Phase 2
12–24 Mo
Replicate to 2–3 additional centres (same geography) · test playbook at scale
Phase 3
24–36 Mo
Expand to 5–10 centres across geographies · begin international MedTech collaborations
Replicability Challenges
  • Hospital buy-in varies significantly by institution culture and leadership
  • Data standards are non-uniform across Indian hospital systems
  • Clinical workflow cultures differ — one playbook may not fit all contexts
  • Regulatory and ethical requirements may vary by state and institution type
Board Discussion
Should the second centre be the same hospital system or a completely different one?
Should partner hospitals become equity stakeholders in a network model?
What is the funding model for multi-centre expansion?
